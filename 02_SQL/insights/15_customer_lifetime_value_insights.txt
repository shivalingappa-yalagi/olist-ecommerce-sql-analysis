15 – CUSTOMER LIFETIME VALUE (CLV) ANALYSIS

Overview: Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) measures total revenue generated
by a customer, average order value, buying frequency, and customer
lifetime. It helps identify high-value customers, repeat buyers, and
overall customer profitability.

Key Metrics: - Total Orders - Total Revenue - Average Order Value
(AOV) - Customer Lifetime (Days between first and last purchase) -
Repeat Category (One-Time Buyer, Occasional Buyer, Moderate Repeat, High
Repeat Customer)

Insights: 1. CLV is highly top-heavy, with some customers generating
very large single-purchase revenues (R$ 6,000–13,000+). 2. Most
customers in the dataset are One-Time Buyers, indicating very low repeat
purchase behavior. 3. Customer lifetime for top buyers is 0 days,
meaning first and last purchases occurred on the same day. 4. Repeat
categories: - One-Time Buyer: Majority - Occasional Buyer: Moderate
count - Moderate Repeat: Small minority - High Repeat: Very rare 5.
Marketplace behavior indicates customers purchase high-ticket items but
do not frequently return.

Strategic Recommendations: - Introduce loyalty and retention programs to
increase repeat buying. - Provide cross-category recommendations based
on customer purchase history. - Use personalized offers to convert
high-value one-time buyers into repeat customers. - Reward high-repeat
customers with exclusive incentives to maintain loyalty. - Improve
post-purchase engagement through emails, coupons, and reminders.
