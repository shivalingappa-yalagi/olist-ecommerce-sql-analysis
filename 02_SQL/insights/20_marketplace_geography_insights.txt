Marketplace Geography Analysis – Key Insights

1. Customer Concentration
   Shows which states have the greatest customer base.
   Helps plan marketing and regional campaigns.

2. Seller Distribution
   States with high seller concentration indicate strong supply presence.
   States with low sellers but high customers indicate imbalance.

3. Revenue by State
   Reveals highest revenue-generating regions.
   Useful for prioritizing logistics, warehousing, and promotions.

4. Demand–Supply Gap
   Comparing seller vs customer count helps identify
   underserved regions and expansion opportunities.

5. Geographic Business Strength
   Strong customer–and–seller presence indicates developed marketplace states.
   Weak presence indicates areas for improvement.

Overall:
Geographical analysis is essential for improving logistics, 
planning market expansion, and optimizing seller–customer distribution.
