Customer Cohort Retention – Key Insights

1. First Purchase Cohorts
   Groups customers by their first purchase month.
   Helps understand which monthly cohorts perform better.

2. Retention Pattern
   Month_Index 0 = First purchase month.
   Following indexes show returning customers.

3. Customer Drop-off
   Large drop-off in early months indicates poor retention.
   Stable retention means strong product-market fit.

4. Returning Customers
   Higher active customers in later months indicate loyal behavior.

5. Business Health
   Cohort retention is a key indicator of business long-term stability.

Overall:
Cohort analysis reveals customer loyalty, repeat purchase behavior,
and retention problems. It is one of the most powerful tools in analytics.
