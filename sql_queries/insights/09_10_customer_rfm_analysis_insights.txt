
# **09_10_Customer RFM Analysis Insights**

This analysis evaluates customers on three behavioral metrics:

* **Recency (R):** How recently the customer purchased
* **Frequency (F):** How often they purchased
* **Monetary (M):** How much revenue they contributed

Each dimension is scored from **1 (worst)** to **5 (best)**.
A classic **11-segment RFM customer model** is applied to obtain meaningful segments.

---

### **Key Observations**

* **Lost customers dominate (40%+)** → These customers have not purchased for a long time and show very low activity.
* **About to Sleep (26.6k)** → Moderate recency but low frequency; require retention campaigns.
* **New Customers (24.7k)** → Large inflow of first-time buyers.
* **Loyal Customers (only 5)** → The loyal base is extremely small, suggesting:

  * low repeat buying behavior,
  * or long repurchase cycles.
* **Lost – High Value (1,778)** → These are urgent targets for reactivation as they previously spent large amounts.

# **2. RFM Grouping Logic Used**

### **Champions**

* R=5, F=5, M=5
* *None found — achieving strongest score requires repeated high-value recent purchases.*

### **Loyal Customers**

* High recency + high frequency
* Only **5 customers** fall in this category → extremely rare repeat buyers.

### **Potential Loyalists**

* Good recency + decent frequency
* **292 customers** moving toward loyalty.

### **Promising**

* **346 customers** with recent purchases but low frequency.

### **New Customers**

* **24,715 customers** with recent first purchase.

### **Need Attention**

* Recency moderate, some frequency
* **750 customers** who can be nudged toward loyalty.

### **About to Sleep**

* Poor recency, low frequency
* **26,675 customers** at risk of churning soon.

### **At Risk**

* Poor recency but previously bought frequently
* **299 customers** require win-back efforts.

### **Hibernating**

* No purchase for a long period
* **1,183 customers** need long-term retention efforts.

### **Lost – High Value**

* Did not buy recently but spent a lot in the past
* **1,778 customers** → very high ROI if reactivated.

### **Lost**

* Very low RFM overall
* **40,435 customers** → largest segment.

# **3. Strategic Business Insights**

### **A. Urgent Win-Back Targets**

* **Lost – High Value (1,778 customers)**
  These customers previously generated major revenue — highly valuable for reactivation.

### **B. Grow the Loyal Base**

* Only **5 Loyal customers** → extremely low.
* Suggests:

  * Long purchase cycles (non-FMCG products)
  * Weak loyalty mechanisms
  * No subscription or membership program

### **C. Strengthen Early Lifecycle**

* The **24,715 New Customers** represent a huge opportunity.
* Onboarding programs, personalized recommendations, and follow-up emails can convert them to repeat buyers.

### **D. Reduce Churn**

* **26,675 About to Sleep**
  Use reminders, promotions, new product arrivals, and retargeting ads.

### **E. Maintain High Satisfaction for Active Segments**

* **Promising (346)** and **Potential Loyalists (292)**
  These are prime candidates for:

  * Exclusive deals
  * Free shipping
  * Priority support
