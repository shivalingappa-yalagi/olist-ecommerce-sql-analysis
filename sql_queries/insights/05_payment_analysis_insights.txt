## **Q1: Count of payments by payment type**

**Key Insights**

* **Credit card** is the overwhelmingly dominant payment method with **76,794 payments**, showing strong consumer reliance on card-based transactions.
* **Boleto** is the second most used method with **19,784 payments**, which is typical in Brazil where boleto bancário is widely adopted.
* **Vouchers** represent a small but notable share (5,775 payments), commonly used for promotions or gift purchases.
* **Debit card usage is very low** (1,529 payments), indicating limited consumer preference.
* **A small number of undefined or inconsistent records** exist (3 + 1 rows).

## **Q3: Average payment value by payment type**

**Key Insights**

* **Credit card payments have the highest average value** at **R$ 163.32**, showing customers prefer credit cards for larger purchases.
* **Boleto** follows with an average of **R$ 145.03**.
* **Debit card** average is **R$ 142.57**, close to boleto.
* **Voucher** transactions are much smaller (R$ 65.70), typical for gift cards or promotional credits.

## **Q7: Monthly payment revenue trend (seasonality)**

**Key Insights**

* Olist revenue shows **consistent month-over-month growth from late 2016 into 2018**.
* **Initial months (2016) have very low revenue**, as the platform was smaller.
* Revenue surges through **2017**, with several peaks:

  * **March 2017**: R$ 419,863
  * **July 2017**: R$ 592,918
  * **December 2017**: R$ 1,194,882 (holiday surge)
* **2018 continues strong** with:

  * **January 2018**: R$ 878,401
  * **March 2018**: R$ 992,643
  * **April 2018**: R$ 1,160,785

This confirms **seasonal spikes**, especially around **year-end holidays**.
