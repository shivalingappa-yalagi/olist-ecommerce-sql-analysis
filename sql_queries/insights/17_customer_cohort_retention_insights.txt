17 – CUSTOMER COHORT RETENTION ANALYSIS

Overview: Cohort retention analysis tracks how customers acquired in a
given month return in future months. A “cohort” is defined by the
customer’s first purchase month. Retention is measured by how many
customers purchase again in subsequent months (month_index 1, 2, 3…).

Key Findings: 1. Olist shows strong customer acquisition growth from
2017 onward. 2. Retention drops sharply after Month 0 (first purchase
month). 3. Very few customers return after 2–3 months, a typical pattern
for marketplace-style e‑commerce. 4. Cohort behavior is consistent
across all months, indicating predictable customer churn.

Cohort Highlights (Month 0 Sizes): 2016-09 – 1 customer 2016-10 – 265
customers 2016-12 – 1 customer 2017-01 – 750 customers 2017-02 – 1,653
customers 2017-03 – 2,617 customers 2017-04 – 2,368 customers (…
continues for remaining months)

Retention Pattern Example (Cohort 2017-02): Month 0: 1653 customers
Month 1: 154 customers Month 2: 117 customers Month 3: 101 customers
Month 4: 80 customers (90%+ churn after Month 0)

Insights: - Olist is primarily a one‑time purchase marketplace. -
Despite thousands of new customers per month, long-term retention
remains low. - Drop-offs are steep but stable across all cohorts. - High
acquisition + low recurrence = strong opportunity for loyalty-building.

Recommendations: - Improve retention through loyalty programs,
repeat-purchase incentives, and personalized marketing. - Analyze
category-level repeat behavior to target high-retention product lines. -
Build automated CRM workflows for Month‑1 and Month‑2 reactivation. -
Use stable cohort trends for accurate forecasting and inventory
planning.
